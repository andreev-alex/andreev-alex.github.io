Design Process

Overall process took me 6 full days from start to finish, until I had a production-ready Figma file.

Research

Problems & Solutions

I started with research about the general coffee-making process because I don't have my own coffee machine. The first question I asked myself about - What makes a coffee maker machine great (good vs bad features)? I use Notion as my default tool for overall process. Based on user research on the Internet I collected several findings and potential solutions for the very first time. Next, I Google how the machines work on a technical level. It could possibly useful for the maintenance alert feature or some other ideas. Then I summarized the whole information in ChatGPT and created a list of key features, that I want to implement in the first MVP design version of the app. Some features I marked as optional, because of tight deadlines

Usage context

Home & Office Environments

  • Make the entire flow of coffee brewing as quick as possible, due lack of time
  • Remote control of the coffee maker, including brewing, scheduling, and stopping
  • Detailed information about the machine's status, maintenance needs, and coffee statistics
  • Customizable settings for different types of coffee and user preferences
  • Notifications and alerts for brewing completion, maintenance needs, and more

I imagined using context to identify areas of the product that need to be taken into account, such as design and product features.

Usage context

Ideas

  • Instant pairing via Bluettoth;
  • Guest mode to skip registration process, and save customers time;
  • Maintenance guide with materials for common issues with a machine;
  • Coffee consuming analytics (for authorized users);
  • Coffee-beans purchase via Apple Pay to save customers time.
  • Remote Brewing: Start and stop brewing coffee remotely.
  • Customizable Coffee Profiles: Save and select different coffee recipes and preferences.
  • Machine Status and Maintenance: Real-time updates on the machine’s status and alerts for maintenance needs.
  • Coffee Statistics: Track usage statistics, such as the number of cups brewed, favorite recipes, and more.
  • Scheduling: Set schedules for automatic brewing at specific times.
  • Notifications: Alerts for brewing completion, low water levels, maintenance reminders, and software updates.
  • Integration with Smart Home Systems: Compatibility with platforms like Google Home, Alexa, and Apple HomeKit.

I have documented the following ideas that emerged from my research.

Prioritize

Then I prioritized my ideas by leveraging an Effort and Impact graph.

Metrics

  • User Engagement: Frequency of app usage and user interactions with different features;
  • Revenue via integrated shop
  • Customer Satisfaction: Feedback and ratings from users about the app and its features;
  • Brewing Statistics: Increase in the number of coffees brewed using remote features and custom profiles;
  • Maintenance Compliance: Reduction in machine breakdowns due to timely maintenance reminders;
  • Smart Home Integration Usage: Number of users connecting the app to smart home systems.

I have defined metrics to measure the success level of our product.

Visual Style

Colors & References

To determine the visual design of a product, I start by selecting three adjectives that best describe the product. Based on this description, I create a basic color palette using the website https://uicolors.app/. Next, I search for suitable typography from Google Fonts or Fontshare. Finally, to define the UI style and find interesting UX patterns, I browse various apps and visual/UX references on Mobbin, or other resources.
